Observations

  • There are more smokers and obese people in the lower poverty range than in the upper poverty range. Lower poverty levels also show a correlation to more healthcare access.
  • An increase in household income did not show a strong increase in health care coverage, but does show fewer smokers and obese people.
  • Smokers and obese people were more prevalent in the middle of the age groups. This could indicate these habits are more likely to occur in mid-life.

Conclusion

Many conclusions can be drawn from the small dataset. In general, it seems there is no direct correlation between household income and good health.
